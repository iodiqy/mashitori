# Hepatobiliary system disorder

### Liver and bile ducts

- **Etiology**
    - **Epidemiologic anamnesis:** blood transfusion,
    occupational hazards, drugs, surgical operations, etc.
    - **Long history of medication:** furadonine, tetracycline,
    anti-tuberculotics (isoniazid, ethambutol), estrogens
    - **Hereditary factor**: benign hyperbilirubinemia,
    Wilson-Konovalov disease (cupper metabolism
    disturbance & accumulation in liver & other organs)
    - **Alcohol abuse**
- **Complaints**
    - **abdominal pain**
    - **dyspepsia** - include ↓ appetite, bitter taste in mouth, eructation, 
    nausea, vomiting, rumbling, constipations or diarrhea
    - **jaundice  -** bilirubin disturbances
    - **skin itching -** accumulation of bile acids in blood
    - **enlargement of the abdomen (ascites) -** 
    destructed blood outflow from intestine via portal vein
    - **fever -** inflammation of the gallbladder and bile ducts, 
    abscess and cancer of the liver, hepatitis, active cirrhosis
- **Pain**
    - **Pain localization?**

        **right hypochondrium** and sometimes in the **epigastrium**

    - **Pain irradiation?**
        - right shoulder
        - scapula
        - interscapular space (in chronic cholecystitis, perihepatitis and pericholecystitis) 
        & **Glisson's capsule** distention when Liver enlarges
    - **What nerve innervates liver and causes pain?**

        right phrenic nerve

        ![Liver,%20pancreas%20and%20bile%2034a69572887247589a8a223ef639b80e/Untitled%201.png](Liver,%20pancreas%20and%20bile%2034a69572887247589a8a223ef639b80e/Untitled%201.png)

    - **In what movements pain is intensified?**
        - **Deep breath** - adhesion of the liver or the gall bladder to the neighboring organs
        - **Changes posture**
        - **Walking**
    - **How colic pain can be relieved?**
        - **warmth** applied to the liver (when there is no fever)
        - administration of **cholino**- and **myospasmolytics** 
        (atropine sulphate, papaverin hydrochloride, etc.)
    - **Cause of pain developing in dyskinesia of the bile ducts?**
        - upset coordination between contractions of the gall bladder and of the Oddi sphincter under the effect of increased tone of the vagus nerve
            - **Biliary dyskinesia**

                is a condition in which the gallbladder does not squeeze well and the bile does not drain out of the gallbladder properly.
